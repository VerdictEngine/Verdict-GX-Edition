Gameplay

How a Case Plays Out

Verdict! is a single-player game where each case is a small puzzle of testimony, evidence, and procedure. You read a witness statement closely, hold the right exhibit until it actually matters, and learn when staying quiet beats raising an objection.

It rewards careful thinking, legal reasoning, and a bit of procedural knowledge rather than fast reflexes. You build the case argument by argument, and the verdict follows from the choices you make.

    History

    From an Idea to 1.3 Million Plays

    How Verdict! Engine got here, one step at a time.

    Late 2024

    The Idea

    Rahul Awasthi starts work on Verdict!, a courtroom game that mixes storytelling with realistic legal mechanics.

    Early 2025

    First Builds

    The Scratch Edition ships as a quick, educational prototype of simple courtroom choices and proves the core idea works.

    2025

    More Platforms

    The Website and Roblox editions arrive: a full browser courtroom, and a 3D virtual trial space to walk around in.

    2025 to 2026

    The GX Edition Takes Off

    The GX Edition catches on and becomes the main release, driving most of the growth.

    2026

    1.3 Million Plays

    Verdict! passes 1,366,800 total plays, and the number keeps climbing.

    The Founder

    Rahul Awasthi

    Creator and Lead Developer, Verdict! Engine

    Rahul started Verdict! Engine in late 2024 with one goal: make legal reasoning fun to play with. He builds Verdict! to mix storytelling with realistic simulation, putting you in the advocate's chair and asking you to think like a litigator.

    It went from a single prototype to a five-edition lineup with over 1.3 million plays. The aim has stayed the same the whole way: every case should make you think, not just wait around.
